Template Methodology for Applying SRAFs

Calibre nmSRAF uses dimension-based and model-based methodologies for cnsraf and mbsraf, respectively.

Calibre® cnSRAF™ defines the placement of SRAFs around features by dimension, and the template defines the geometry and placement of the SRAFs. Calibre® mbSRAF™ defines placement of SRAFs by field analysis adhering to lithographic models.

You can use Calibre OPCsbar and Calibre cnSRAF together. In Calibre OPCsbar, rules define SRAF placement by classifying edges by their width and distance to other features. An SRAF of specified geometry is placed at an offset from each edge in every classification. To blend scattering bar and SRAF generation you use Calibre OPCsbar and Calibre cnSRAF together, respectively. Refer to “Calibre cnSRAF and Calibre OPCsbar Integration” for more information.

The proper template specification is dependent upon the optical configuration of the lithographic system. Asymmetric optical source configurations typically require asymmetrical Calibre nmSRAF templates. Resolution enhancement processes ensure the most effective arrangement of SRAFs for all isolated vias are consistent. Templates are repeatedly applied to each of the features in a layout. However, vias are rarely isolated and SRAF placement must be determined by applying additional prioritized templates, SRAF mask rule checks (MRC), and conflict resolution schemes.
