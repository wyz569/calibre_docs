Introduction to Calibre nmSRAF

Calibre® nmSRAF™ uses a template-defined method to insert sub-resolution assist features into a design. Calibre nmSRAF includes dimension- and model-based SRAF generation tools. Sub-Resolution Assist Features (SRAFs) are slivers of chrome strategically placed on the mask to sharpen printable images. The generated SRAFs remain too small to print.

Calibre nmSRAF is used for advanced nodes where stronger off-axis sources can induce proximity effects that are difficult to compensate for with Calibre® OPCsbar™. Calibre nmSRAF and Calibre OPCsbar can be used in tandem as required for design and illumination.

The template-based approach used in Calibre nmSRAF provides the flexibility to capture the shape requirements of Calibre® pxOPC™, an inverse lithography SRAF insertion engine. This flexibility of shape definition, along with the speed of template application, brings the lithographic power of inverse lithography to the manufacturing flow in a cost effective manner.

You combine Calibre nmSRAF with SRAF optimization tools available in Calibre® nmOPC™ to tune the SRAF to each local environment in a design. These optimizations use many of the same placement and sizing algorithms as Calibre pxOPC. The final SRAF placement and sizing are as effective as an initial Calibre pxOPC insertion, but in less time and with complete mask rule check (MRC) control included.
