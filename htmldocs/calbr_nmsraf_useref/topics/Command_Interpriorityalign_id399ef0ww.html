interpriorityalign

Cleanup Keywords

Aligns SRAFs of different priorities during cleanup. Used by RET SBAR and RET MBSRAF.

Usage

interpriorityalign value [centermove value] [spacerule 1 | -1]

Arguments

value

A required positive floating point number. The value is the maximum distance lower‑priority SRAFs can be moved to align with higher priority SRAFs.

centermove value

An optional positive floating point number. This is the maximum amount of movement allowed for center SRAFs. The default value is the interpriorityalign value.

spacerule 1 | ‑1

An optional argument, either 1 or ‑1. A value of -1 means alignment will drive SRAFs towards target shapes. A value of 1 drives alignment away from target shapes. The default value is 1.

Description

An optional keyword specified only by priority to align and optionally merge SRAFs of different priorities during SRAF cleanup. To simultaneously permit merging, you must specify the lineendmerge option. When you specify the interpriorityalign keyword for a given priority, all SRAFs of that priority are aligned with SRAFs of higher priorities only.

Note:

The interpriorityalign keyword is incompatible with cleanupversion 0 and results in a compile-time error. The interpriorityalign keyword is incompatible with maxlength.

The fundamental alignment location rules include:

  • The initial position of SRAFs are given greater emphasis during alignment processing.

  • Symmetric alignment solutions are given greater emphasis during alignment processing.

  • The widths of SRAFs may be adjusted by interpriorityalign during alignment and line‑end merging.

For alignment of SRAFs with the different priority, these conditions must be satisfied:

  • The orientation must be the same.

  • Diagonal or square SRAFs are not supported.

  • A minimum distance is established from SRAFs to target shapes through specification of minfeaturespace.

  • SRAFs cannot align if they are shielded by a target shape. SRAFs are not shielded where their long-end projections are overlapping. The shielded area is determined by the bounding box of the two SRAFs shown; two edges of the shielded area touch the lineend of SRAFs and the other two edges touch the boundary of the shielded area. In the example, the red box is the shielded area of the two green SRAFs. The SRAFs are shielded by the blue target shape where it appears within the shielded area, and therefore no alignment is performed.

  • In the example, widths of SRAFs are 0.02 um. The first three SRAFs ascending from the left can be aligned and the three SRAFs descending from the right can be aligned together, but all four of them cannot be aligned into a single SRAF. No alignment scenarios exceeding the SRAF widths would be handled by interpriorityalign, but other cleanup steps may be available to resolve this situation.

  • The lineend distance between two SRAFs must be less than or equal to the specified lineendmerge value. The lineendmerge keyword is required for interpriorityalign to work. A parsing error is issued if lineendmerge is not specified. There is no default value for lineendmerge.

  • Siemens EDA recommends values of interpriorityalign to be less than maximum width of its associated priority. If the value exceeds this amount, a warning message is issued.

The interpriorityalign keyword demonstrates these behavioral characteristics:

  • The value specified for interpriorityalign acts as the search distance for the alignment of eligible SRAF.

  • Only two SRAFs of the same width can be merged.

  • The maximum movement for a center-placed SRAF is equivalent to the value specified for interpriorityalign.

  • During alignment, SRAFs are moved away from target shapes.

  • The widths of SRAFs may be adjusted by intrapriorityalign during alignment and line‑end merging.

Note:

Siemens EDA recommends always specifying interpriorityalign by priority. A warning message is issued to the transcript when interpriorityalign is specified globally.

Examples

In Figure 1, all SRAFs generated by the rectedge template specifying prior 2 would be moved to align with the SRAFs generated by prior 1 (because lineendmerge is also specified, the prior 2 SRAFs would also be merged with those of prior 1).

Example 1 — interpriorityalign Code

rectEdge tag tag1 extension 0.020 width 0.040 offset 0.060 prior 1
rectEdge tag tag2 extension 0.020 width 0.040 offset 0.080 prior 2
priority 2 lineendmerge 0.020 interpriorityalign 0.030
