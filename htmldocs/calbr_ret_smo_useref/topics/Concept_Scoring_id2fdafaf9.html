Scoring

A quick way to compare the results of many sessions is to calculate a “goodness” score.

Scoring is based on your decisions about what constitutes optimal results. You enter the minimum acceptable values for items such as DOF, and the maximum acceptable process variation, and also assign weights. Based on this, the RET Selection compares the measurements on gauges and computes an aggregate score for each source.

To enter the decision values, open a plotting window. Below the plots and above the gauge table is Edit Layout Spec (). This button opens the Gauge Property Spec Editor dialog box, where you can enter the values and assign per-gauge weights.

After you have entered the values you care about in the Gauge Property Spec Editor, the plots include markers to indicate the thresholds you set. The table at the bottom of the Inter-Plots window also populates the Layout-Spec Delta columns, including the aggregate score in the final column.

You can further tune the “goodness” score using the Config Score button (), in the same menu bar as Edit Layout Spec.
