Comparison of Centerline and Effective Length Methods for Finding Path Length

The path length of polygons in a net can be computed by two different methods: centerline method and effective length method.

  • Finding the Length of a Net Path Connected to Gate: Effective Length Method

    Estimates the effective length of each polygon in the path using an equation.

  • Finding the Length of a Net Path Connected to Gate: Centerline Method

    Finds the approximate centerlines of the polygons in the path and sums the total length of the centerlines. Edges that are less than a specified minimum edge length are considered corners and are handled separately, so that lengths are not counted twice at corners.

Figure 1 compares the length estimates for the two methods when applied to several shapes. The lines are approximations of the length for each method. For the centerline estimation method, a minimum edge length of 1.0 is used, and a maximum line width of 3.0. See the derivation in “Finding the Length of a Net Path Connected to Gate: Centerline Method” to see how these values are used.

The three shapes in the top half of the figure are Manhattan shapes with a uniform width and no stubs, and the length estimates from the two methods are the same. The shapes in the bottom half of the figure have short stubs, non-uniform width, or angled edges, and the length estimates from the two methods are different. For the centerline method, a different value of the minimum edge length would give different results in some cases.
