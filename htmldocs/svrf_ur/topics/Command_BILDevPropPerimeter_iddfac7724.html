perimeter

Device property computation language function

Returns the total length of the perimeter of the shapes for the specified pin or layer.

Usage

PERIMeter(pin_or_layer)

Arguments

pin_or_layer

A required pin name or device layer as described in “Pin and Layer References”.

Return Values

Floating-point number in meters.

Description

The perimeter() function gives the total perimeter of a shape regardless of any other layer that may interact with the shape.

The related perimeter functions return the length of portions of perimeters on one pin or layer as they relate to shapes on a second pin or layer. All values are assumed to be in meters by default.

Consider two overlapping shapes on layers A and B as shown in the following figure.

Figure 1. Perimeter Relationships

The perimeter of A can be divided into a maximum of three portions depending on its relation to the shape on layer B. These three portions have been labeled with numbers as follows:

    1: Perimeter of A is strictly INSIDE B.

    2: Perimeters of A and B COINCIDE.

    3: Perimeter of A is strictly OUTSIDE B.

Portion 2 can be further subdivided into two portions in certain configurations. These have been labeled as follows:

    2a: Interior of A adjacent to the coincident edge lies INSIDE B.

    2b: Interior of A adjacent to the coincident edge lies OUTSIDE B.
