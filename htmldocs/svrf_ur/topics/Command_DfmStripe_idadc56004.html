DFM Stripe

Layer operation

License: Calibre YieldEnhancer

Generates an output layer with stripes of fixed width. The stripes are placed within the shapes on a polygon layer.

Usage

DFM STRIPE layer width separation [HORIZONTAL | VERTICAL]
[DISTRIBUTE mode]

Arguments

layer

A required polygon layer. The stripes are placed inside this layer.

width

A required argument giving the width of the stripes in user units. The stripe width is fixed.

separation

A required argument giving the separation between the stripes in user units. The DISTRIBUTE mode setting also affects the separation between the stripes.

HORIZONTAL | VERTICAL

An optional keyword that specifies the stripe orientation. The default is HORIZONTAL.

DISTRIBUTE mode

An optional keyword and integer that specifies how the stripes are distributed. The mode can be one of the following integers:

  • 0 — Stripes are placed starting at the lower left corner of the fill region. Any remaining space is placed at the top of the fill region for horizontal stripes, or at the right edge of the fill region for vertical stripes. This is the default.

  • 1 — Similar to mode 0, but the first stripe is placed half a stripe below or to the left of the fill region boundary, with any extra space at the top or right edge.

  • 2 — Stripes are placed in the center of the fill region, with any extra space distributed as equally as possible at the outer edges of the fill region (top and bottom for horizontal stripes, left and right sides for vertical stripes).

  • 3 — Stripes are placed at the outer edges of the fill region, with extra space placed in the center of the fill region. If the stripe count is odd, the extra space is distributed evenly around the middle stripe.

  • 4 — The stripe spacing s is uniform, but the specified separation is considered a minimum spacing. The maximum number of stripes is found for a fixed spacing s >= separation. The stripes are placed in the center of the fill region, with extra space distributed as evenly as possible at the outer edges of the fill region (as with mode 2).

  • 5 — Similar to mode 4, but stripes are placed first at the outer edges of the fill region, as with mode 3. Extra space is handled as with mode 3.

  • 6 — The specified separation is considered a minimum spacing. The stripes are distributed as uniformly as possible throughout the region.

    Two stripes are first placed at the outer boundaries of the fill region (top and bottom for horizontal stripes, left and right sides for vertical stripes). The remaining stripes are placed as uniformly as possible by adjusting the spacing s between stripes, where s >= separation and the spacing s between different stripes varies by at most 1 dbu. If only one stripe can be placed in the fill region, it is centered within the region.

Description

DFM Stripe places stripes of fixed width within a specified polygon layer. The distribution and spacing of stripes is controlled by the separation parameter and the “DISTRIBUTE mode” keyword set.

Examples

Create horizontal stripes with DISTRIBUTE 2. 

stripes_2 = DFM STRIPE fill_region 0.012 0.006 HORIZONTAL DISTRIBUTE 2
