DFM Grow

Layer operation

License: Calibre YieldEnhancer

Creates a derived polygon layer by expanding polygons on the input layer. Expansion occurs by a specified step size until a target area or iteration count is met. Expanded edges must meet a specified spacing constraint. This operation is a non-node-preserving layer constructor.

Usage

DFM GROW layer
TARGETAREA area_target
MINWIDTH min_width
SPACE space_value
STEP step_size
ITERATIONS count

Arguments

layer

A required original or derived polygon layer. Polygons from this layer that meet the criteria of this operation are expanded.

TARGETAREA area_target

A required keyword and floating-point number in square user units that specify the desired minimum area for each polygon. Polygons on layer with an area greater than or equal to area_target are not expanded. Polygon expansion stops when the polygon area is equal to or greater than area_target or after count expansion steps, whichever comes first.

MINWIDTH min_width

A required keyword and floating-point number that specify the minimum width, in user units, that an edge must have before expansion is applied. This criteria is applied at each iteration.

At least one vertical or horizontal edge of the polygon must be longer than min_width for expansion to occur.

SPACE space_value

A required keyword and floating-point number that specify the minimum edge spacing, in user units, to be enforced during polygon expansion. This spacing is always enforced between expanded layer edges, even if this prevents polygon expansion.

STEP step_size

A required keyword and floating-point number that specify the increment, in user units, by which to expand layer polygons.

ITERATIONS count

A required keyword and positive integer that specify the maximum number of expansion steps the algorithm applies. Polygon expansion stops when the polygon area is equal to or greater than area_target or after count expansion steps, whichever comes first.

The count should be less than 60.

Description

Expands polygons on the input layer until a minimum area condition is met, or until a specified number of growth iterations is met. Expanded edges must meet a specified spacing constraint. DFM Grow creates a derived polygon layer by applying alternate horizontal and vertical Expand Edge operations. The output from DFM Grow may be different in flat versus hierarchical runs. DFM Grow only expands edges that are orthogonal to the databases axes—angled edges are never expanded.

DFM Grow is useful when you want to grow polygons subject to a spacing rule, and uniform expansion is not necessary. The SPACE and MINWIDTH keywords prevent spacing, notch, and width violations in the expanded polygons.

For each polygon on layer with an area less than area_target, the horizontal edges are expanded first, then the vertical edges. To be expanded, an edge on the input layer polygon must meet these conditions at each expansion step:

  • The edge is longer than min_width

  • The expansion does not cause the edge to violate the space_value spacing constraint

The expansion is repeated until the resulting polygon area is greater than or equal to area_target or count iterations have been performed.

Polygons on layer with an area greater than or equal to area_target or with all vertical and horizontal edges shorter than or equal to min_width are not expanded, and are copied to the output layer.

Examples

This example grows polygons with a step size of 0.25 user units for a maximum of 4 iterations, or until the resulting polygon area is greater than 10 user units squared. Expanded edges must have a length greater than 2 user units, and be separated by 1 user unit.

LAYER layer1 1
out_dfm_grow = DFM GROW layer1 
         TARGETAREA 10
         MINWIDTH  2
         SPACE 1
         STEP .25
         ITERATIONS 4
Figure 1. DFM GROW

These are the initial lengths of the input edges:

A1: 0.707

B1: 1.0

C1: 0.50

A2: 0.75

B2: 2.25

C2: 0.75

A3: 2.75

B3: 1.00

C3: 0.50

A4: 1.25

B4: 2.25

C4: 0.75

A5: 2.25

 

 

The expansion result demonstrates these items:

  • Edge A1 is not expanded because it is at an angle. Angled edges are not expanded, regardless of length.

  • Edge A2 is not expanded because it is too short, even after expansion of edge A3.

  • Edge A4 does not expand until the expansion of edges A3 and A5 increases the length of edge A4 to more than the min_width of 2.

  • Edges B1 and B3 do not expand until the expansion of edges B2 and B4 increases the length of edges B1 and B3 to more than the min_width of 2.

  • Expansion of edges B1 and B4 are limited by the spacing rule.

  • Edges C1, C2, C3, and C4 are not expanded because they are less than the min_width of 2. The polygon is copied to the output layer.

  • The expansion stops after 4 iterations, or a maximum edge expansion of 1.0.
