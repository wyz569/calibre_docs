Overlapping Regions

When regions overlap, the criteria for each region must be met.

The following figure shows an example with overlapping Critical and Don’t Care regions.

Figure 1. Region Overlap

In effect, the overlap of a Critical and Don’t Care region functions as Critical region. If the overlap is part of a larger Critical region, the Critical region can be satisfied by a shape anywhere in the Critical region.

Keep Out regions are not recommended—see “Keep Out Regions”. However, if Keep Out Regions are used and overlap with a Don’t Care or Critical region, there can be no layout shape in the overlap region. This is because both region criteria must be satisfied. Critical and Keep Out regions cannot be completely coincident, because both conditions cannot be satisfied.
