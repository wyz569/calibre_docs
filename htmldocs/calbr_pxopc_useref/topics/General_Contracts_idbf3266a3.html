Job Contracts

For a correctly optimized mask, every job must deliver certain measurable results (the contract). A bad result can be traced back to the first job that does not fulfill its contract.
Table 1. Contracts Summary

Job

Contract

Open

  • Printed shapes at nominal conditions.

  • Contacts deviate no more than 30% and lines no more than 40% from the target critical dimension (CD).

If the contract is not met, see “Fixing Open Job Problems”.

Decorate

Decoratedarksraf

  • At least two rows of assist features are generated.

  • SRAFs are robust and in dark areas.

  • Main features print, and no more than 25% of SRAFs print.

If the contract is not met, see “Fixing Decorate Job Problems”.

Unclose

This job should only be used if Decorate is not meeting its contract and using negative SRAFs.

  • Main features print, though holes in the contours are allowed because negative SRAFs are present.

  • Printing shapes are on target within 40% of target CD.

Correct

  • Printing shapes are on target within 8% of CD.

  • Printing SRAFs from Decorate input have been mostly eliminated.

If the contract is not met, see “Fixing Correct Job Problems”.

CDOF_Curvilinear

EL_Curvilinear

  • The best possible common depth of focus or dose latitude.

  • No extra printing occurs.

  • No catastrophic failures on main features.

Refine

Finetune (LPE)

  • No SRAFs print.

  • Areas marked with weight layers are printing on target within fractions of a nanometer.

If the contract is not met, see “Fixing Refine Job Problems”

LMRC

Detail

  • The output mask is strictly Manhattan.

  • No extra printing occurs.

  • MRC rules are obeyed.

If the contract is not met, see “Fixing LMRC Job Problems” or “Fixing Detail Job Problems”.

Detailhybrid

Detailrectsraf

Same as Detail job contract except the output mask.

  • The Detailhybrid output mask has curvilinear main features with Manhattan SRAFs.

  • The Detailrectsraf output mask has Manhattan main features and rectangular SRAFs.

CDOF

EL

  • The best possible common depth of focus or dose latitude.

  • The output mask is strictly Manhattan.

  • No extra printing occurs.

  • No catastrophic failures on main features.

  • MRC rules are obeyed.

If the contract is not met, see “Fixing CDOF Job Problems” or “Fixing EL Job Problems”.

To debug results, check intermediate layers to identify the first job that does not deliver the required output. (See the “Fixing…” sections in “Calibre pxOPC Configuration and Tuning” for step-by-step debugging instructions.)
