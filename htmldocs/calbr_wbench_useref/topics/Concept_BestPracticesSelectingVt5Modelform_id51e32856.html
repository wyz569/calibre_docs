Best Practices for Selecting a VT5 Modelform

This section explains some best practices for selecting a VT5 modelform. They can be applied in VT5 Center or with the modelflow_v1 command.

To set a modelform:

  • In VT5 Center, in the V5,in tab, click the Modelform button.

  • In modelflow, use the stage0 modelform argument.

In general, selecting modelforms is a trade-off between data fitting, prediction, and runtime performance.

  • Start your calibration trials with a simple modelform that uses only first and second order terms, then increase terms to improve data fitting.

  • Stop when you find a modelform that provides a satisfactory fit.

  • When selecting a resist model form (also known as a Threshold Polynomial):

    • Choosing modelform 84 usually produces high model accuracy.

    • Choosing modelform 80 or 81 is a good compromise between speed and accuracy.

  • When selecting an etch model form (also known as a Bias Polynomial):

    • Choosing modelforms 121, 160, or 161 produces high model accuracy.

    • Choosing modelform 122 or 162 is a good compromise between speed and accuracy.

    • Do not use more than three kernels.

    • Do not use the factor coefficient.

  • Do not mix Gaussian and tophat kernels in one polynomial.
