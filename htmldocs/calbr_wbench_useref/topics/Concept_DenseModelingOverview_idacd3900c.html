Dense Modeling Overview

Dense modeling refers to two separated types of models: CM1 and VEB.

CM1 Modeling Overview

Compact Model 1 (CM1) is a Siemens EDA resist model.

CM1 provides a dense simulation (as opposed to the sparse VT5 simulation), simulating printability at evenly-spaced grid points instead of a limited number of simulation control sites. It accounts for acid and base neutralization and diffusion lengths during the exposure, baking, and resist development phases.

CM1 models let you predict the results of your total RET solution by providing a full-area process simulation.

VEB Modeling Overview

Variable Etch Bias (VEB) is a Siemens EDA model.

VEB is designed to model the effects of etch bias or other biasing processes for dense modeling purposes. The VEB modeling phase is optional, and can only be performed when the additional post-etch data is available.
