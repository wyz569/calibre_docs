Calibre LITHOview and Calibre WORKbench Overview

The Calibre® LITHOview™ and Calibre® WORKbench™ tools are designed for the resolution enhancement technology (RET) user. They add simulation and modeling capabilities to the Calibre® DESIGNrev™ layout viewing and editing tool.

(You should also become familiar with Calibre DESIGNrev, described separately in the Calibre DESIGNrev User’s Manual.)

Tip

Calibre LITHOview versus Calibre WORKbench: Calibre LITHOview is a light version of Calibre WORKbench that includes the simulation tools, but not the model creation tools.

Calibre LITHOview Functionality

Calibre LITHOview is a model and design-based simulator with multiple uses.

  • Simulating aerial and resist images with supplied optical and process models (resist and etch models together are known as process models).

  • Visually inspecting fragmentation schemes for OPC.

  • Testing the simulated effects of small modifications to your design file.

Calibre WORKbench Functionality

Calibre WORKbench adds a model creation tool suite to Calibre LITHOview. Use Calibre WORKbench to create the optical and process models used in simulations.

  • Optical models via the Optical Model Tool and an underlying configurable optical model file.

  • Process (resist and etch) models through the VT5 Center (replacing the Model Flow GUI) and CM1 Center tools.

  • Test pattern creation tools, for use in taking real lithography measurements on test chips.

  • Performing Optical Proximity Correction (OPC) on loaded design files
