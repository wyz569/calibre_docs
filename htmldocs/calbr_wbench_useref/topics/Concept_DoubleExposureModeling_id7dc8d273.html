Double Exposure Modeling

Running OPC for double exposure lithography requires a model that employs two optical models, one for each mask exposure, plus a resist model and possibly an etch model. Modeling double exposure configurations is supported by CM1 Center and modelflow_v2.

Double Dipole Lithography (DDL) is the most common example of double exposure lithography; it separates a target pattern into two masks:

  • one with primarily horizontal features corresponding to a strong dipole illumination oriented along the y-axis

  • one with primarily vertical features corresponding to a strong dipole illumination oriented along the x-axis

Consequently, two optical models are required, one for each dipole illumination source. Each must be calibrated separately.
