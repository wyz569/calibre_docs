beamfocus

Parent topic: Optical Parameters File Format

Input for: opticsgen, modelflow (Version 1), modelflow_v2

Defines the position of best focus in the ambient medium.

Usage

beamfocus zval

Arguments

zval

A required argument specifying the position in microns into (or out of) the film stack relative to the ambient and first-film interface.

  • A negative number indicates that the best focus in the ambient occurs outside of the film stack.

  • A positive value indicates that the best focus in the ambient occurs inside of the film stack.

Description

A required Z-axis parameter for thin-film models that defines the position into (or out of) the film stack at which the best focus plane in the ambient medium (air or water) would occur.

The beamfocus parameter is NOT the location of best focus of the image formed inside the resist. The position of best focus in the presence of the resist film stack will in general be at z greater than 0 and can only be determined from the simulation.

Assuming that there is no TARC layer, as a rule of thumb, the position of best focus will be at approximately n_ambient/n_resist * d when the imaginary part of the resist refractive index is small and the BARC effectively cancels reflections. n_ambient is the real part of the wafer immersion ambient, n_resist is the real part of the resist refractive index, and d is the distance between the top of the film stack and the reference focal plane.

For TARC cases, the formula is:

  • n_ambient / n_tarc*d, for d < t_tarc

  • n_ambient / n_tarc*t_tarc + n_ambient/n_resist*(d-t_tarc), for t_tarc < d < (t_tarc+ t_resist)

Extrapolate as necessary for additional films.

When using beamfocus, the defocuslevels are understood as being relative to the ambient/first-film interface.
