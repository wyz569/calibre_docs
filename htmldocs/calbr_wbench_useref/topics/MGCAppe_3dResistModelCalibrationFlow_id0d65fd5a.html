3D Resist Model Calibration Flow

3D resist modeling is usually recommended at 22nm technology nodes or below. At this node size, the vertical height of resist structures is relevant to the final design. It is typically needed when there a low k1 imaging situation. A 3D resist model may need vertical diffusion and z-dependent terms in CM1 to be more accurate. A model without these terms can still predict a resist profile, but the accuracy of that profile may not be good enough. The presence of strong standing waves generally indicates a resist model benefits from 3D.

A 3D resist model can be made without diffusion. It is calibrated with profile data and has additional 3D features to capture the variation in z (height). Together this is usually sufficient to predict the resist profile.

At larger node sizes, the resist pre-etch and resist loss are usually negligible.
