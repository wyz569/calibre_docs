Comparison of Extraction Types

A design can be extracted in several different ways. Which one is right for you depends on your design stage and what you intend to do with the netlist.

Depending on your design stage, different extraction types may be more accurate. For example, flat extraction preserves all the interaction between components, but an extraction with xcells allows you to skip pre-characterized cells or devices. Similarly, if some parts of the design are to be supplied later, a flat extraction provides misleading results compared to a gate-level extraction with xcells for the missing parts.

The other consideration is the size of the produced data and netlist. Large designs may require several gigabytes of memory for the PHDB and PDB. Flat extraction requires more space than any form of hierarchical extraction.
