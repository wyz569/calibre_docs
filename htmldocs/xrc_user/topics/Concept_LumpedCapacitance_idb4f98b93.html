Lumped Capacitance

With lumped capacitance extraction, all capacitance for a net is modeled as one parasitic capacitor between the net and substrate. Depending on the command line options, the parasitic capacitor may include the effects of coupled capacitance to another net with the effect lumped to ground. Resistance is not modeled.

Figure 1 shows a simplified layout of two cells with interconnect. For lumped capacitance, or Lumped C, two possible models can be extracted based on a command line option in the formatting step.

Figure 1. Simplified Layout for Lumped Capacitance

Figure 2 shows how the capacitors are represented in the model when the -g command line option is used in the formatting step:

calibre -xrc -pdb -c 
calibre -xrc -fmt -c -g

With this option, the coupled capacitance is added to each intrinsic capacitor, extracted from the net to ground.

Figure 2. Lumped Capacitance With Coupled Capacitor Added to Intrinsic Capacitors

Figure 3 shows how the extracted capacitors are represented in the model without the -g command line option:

calibre -xrc -pdb -c 
calibre -xrc -fmt -c

Without the -g option, the couple capacitor is present as a separate device in the net model.

Figure 3. Lumped Capacitance With Coupled Capacitor
